# 1. Project Overview

## Title

**White Island / Project Deep Current**

## Current Format

The current prototype is built with:

* HTML
* CSS
* JavaScript

Current main files:

```text
index.html
styles.css
app.js
events.js
```

## Genre Direction

The project is a hybrid of:

* relationship ADV
* loop-based narrative structure
* psychological choice game
* survival phase
* community stabilization phase
* state formation / national development phase

However, it should not become a standard survival game or a generic strategy simulation.

The emotional core must remain connected to **Wata**, **Minato**, memory, dependency, fear, and the way personal choices become social systems.

---

# 2. Core Concept

The core of this game is:

> A small, intimate fear becomes a system.
> A system becomes a country.
> A country remembers the relationship that created it.

In Japanese:

> 小さな個人的な不安が、やがて仕組みになる。
> 仕組みは共同体になり、国家になる。
> その国は、最初にそれを生んだ二人の関係を覚えている。

This is not a game where kindness is simply punished.

The intended structure is:

> Minato's kindness does reach Wata.
> But kindness alone cannot resolve everything.
> What remains unresolved may later become dependency, ideology, fear, hope, infrastructure, law, or national identity.

The game asks:

* What happens when a friend's fear is taken seriously?
* What happens when care becomes a habit?
* What happens when a private relationship becomes a social structure?
* What happens when an attempt to save someone becomes a country?

---

# 3. Main Characters

## Wata

Wata is Minato's childhood friend.

Wata should be written as:

* sensitive to the pain of the world
* genuinely kind
* unstable, but not foolish
* prone to light lies and avoidance
* afraid of disappearance, collapse, and being left behind
* drawn to large-scale anxieties such as prophecy, disaster, war, poverty, and the end of the world
* someone who hides fear through jokes, lightness, or obviously weak excuses

Wata must not become simply:

* a helpless victim
* a generic unstable friend
* a conspiracy theorist caricature
* a villain
* a pure saint
* a comic relief character
* a mere plot device

Wata's important contradiction:

> Wata wants to save the world, but struggles to endure ordinary social pressure.

This contradiction is central.

---

## Minato

Minato is the protagonist.

Minato should be written as:

* someone who wants to help Wata
* someone who cannot fully separate from Wata
* someone whose kindness is real but not pure
* someone who may feel needed, useful, or special beside Wata
* someone who translates Wata's fear into action, preparation, systems, and eventually institutions

Minato must not become simply:

* a perfect savior
* a cold manipulator
* a generic silent protagonist
* a teacher-like moral guide
* a purely rational manager
* a passive observer of Wata's collapse

Minato's important contradiction:

> Minato wants to save Wata, but may also want to remain necessary to Wata.

This contradiction should remain subtle, not loudly explained.

## Chapter 3 - Island / State Formation Skeleton

Chapter 3 currently exists as a skeleton with three phases:

### Survival Phase

* 1 turn = 1 day
* goal: survive
* resources include food, water, safety, trust, anxiety, clams, bait, bottles, etc.
* includes first-loop survival logic

### Stabilization Phase

* 1 turn = 1 week
* goal: establish a stable community
* provisional systems include shelter, roles, rules, resources, trust, and leadership

### Development Phase

* 1 turn = 1 month
* goal: develop the country
* provisional systems include capital, diplomacy, record, anxiety, and national direction

Current Chapter 3 elements include:

* Wata and Minato waking on a reef
* Isora arriving
* clam gathering
* bait preparation
* fishing
* water scarcity
* safety management
* first-loop survival structure
* memory items used as survival resources
* Wata's declaration toward community leadership
* provisional transition to national development

Important implemented emotional mechanic:

> Items from earlier life, such as a diary or Wata's keychain, may become survival materials.

This is important and should not be treated as a cheap shock gimmick.
It should express the idea that memory itself becomes material under survival pressure.

# 5. Chapter Structure

## Chapter 1 - Ordinary School Life

Chapter 1 should remain grounded in ordinary life.

Focus on:

* school
* club activities
* awkward conversations
* small lies
* avoidance
* Wata's lightness
* Minato's responses
* small seeds of later systems

Chapter 1 should not directly explain:

* the white island
* state building
* final catastrophe mechanics
* countdown systems
* late-game national endings
* exact post-collapse geography

Chapter 1 should feel ordinary on the surface, with small wrong notes underneath.

---

## Deep Current

The Deep Current is a loop-space and memory-space.

It should feel:

* sacred
* cold
* submerged
* unsettling
* not entirely physical
* connected to memory, seeds, and repetition

It is not just a menu.

It is a symbolic layer between lives / loops / structures.

---

## Chapter 2 - Translation Phase

Chapter 2 is the bridge between childhood and the island/state phase.

It should show how Chapter 1 seeds become adult-life consequences.

Possible formats:

* short novel fragments
* timeline entries
* messages
* news clippings
* letters
* records
* limited major choices

Chapter 2 should not become a huge second game unless explicitly decided.

Its purpose is causal explanation, not mechanical expansion for its own sake.

---

## Chapter 3 - Survival, Stabilization, Development

Chapter 3 should unfold through three scales of time.

### Survival Phase

* 1 turn = 1 day
* the question is: "Can we survive today?"

### Stabilization Phase

* 1 turn = 1 week
* the question is: "Can this become a community?"

### Development Phase

* 1 turn = 1 month
* the question is: "What kind of country is this becoming?"

Chapter 3 must not feel like a completely separate game.

Resource management must remain connected to:

* Wata
* Minato
* memory
* notes
* records
* previous choices
* old objects
* relationship debt

# 7. Key Systems

## Stats

Current stats include protagonist-related and friend-related values such as:

* intelligence
* stamina
* popularity
* depth
* luck
* condition
* trust
* friendDistance
* friendAnxiety
* friendIndependence

These are currently displayed in the side panel.

They should not become the only meaning of the game.
They are tools for expressing relationship and direction, not the emotional core itself.

---

## Seeds

Seeds represent thematic and psychological directions.

Current examples include:

* groupParticipation
* twoPersonWorld
* groupFear
* groupHope
* scienceTechnology
* expression
* prophecy
* clubWithdrawal
* friendIndependence
* leadership
* memoryBait
* lurebait

Seeds should be treated as:

* memory traces
* thematic tendencies
* possible future systems
* routes toward later community/state forms

Do not create excessive one-off seeds without documenting them.

---

## Flags

Flags represent specific memories or event traces.

They are useful for flavor, logs, and conditional branching.

However, avoid uncontrolled flag explosion.

Major branching should prefer aggregated or categorized state values when possible.

---

## Deep Current Carryover

Deep Current allows seeds to be reviewed and carried over.

This supports the loop structure.

Carryover should feel like memory, not just a gameplay bonus.

---

## Titles

Titles currently function like achievement / identity markers.

They should reflect how Minato related to Wata across a life.

They must not become merely collectible medals.

# 8. Chapter 3 Resource Direction

Chapter 3 resources should change by phase.

## Survival Phase

Core resources should remain concrete:

* food
* water
* safety
* body condition / stamina
* trust
* anxiety

The player should feel the immediacy of survival.

## Stabilization Phase

Resources should become communal:

* food supply
* water source stability
* shelter
* labor
* roles / rules
* trust
* anxiety
* technology seed
* external contact

The player should feel the shift from survival to community.

## Development Phase

Resources should become national:

* population
* food base
* water infrastructure
* urban foundation
* technology
* institutions
* culture
* defense
* diplomacy / trade
* legitimacy / consent
* anxiety
* hope
* records

The most project-specific resources are:

* anxiety
* trust
* hope
* records

These should remain central.

# 9. Tone and UI Direction

The project atmosphere should remain:

* cold
* quiet
* monochromatic
* literary
* intimate before it becomes political
* sacred but unsettling

Avoid turning it into:

* loud anime apocalypse
* heroic survival fantasy
* pure resource optimization
* cheerful management game
* standard kingdom builder
* lore-heavy exposition dump

## UI Metaphor

The UI should be based on:

* desk
* notebook
* map
* record
* paper
* messages
* survival documents
* handwritten traces

Chapter 1 may evoke a school desk.

Chapter 3 may evoke a survival desk or command table.

The player should feel:

> I am not simply managing a country.
> I am managing the consequences of a relationship that became a country.

* event IDs

* event order
* approved event text
* the current basketball route
* Deep Current behavior
* debug tools

* changed files
* added files

* state keys changed or added

# 11. Do Not Do

Do not:

* explain the white island too early
* expose Chapter 3 mechanics in Chapter 1
* make Wata merely annoying, unstable, or foolish
* make Minato a perfect savior
* make every kind choice a trap
* make resource management erase the emotional core
* let Gemini's harshest suggestions define the final tone without emotional correction
* let Codex / Claude Code invent major story text freely
* change basketball back to baseball without explicit human decision
* turn Chapter 2 into a huge simulation without explicit human decision
* turn Chapter 3 into a generic strategy game
* delete existing event content without permission

# 13. Final Principle

When in doubt, protect this core:

> A small, intimate fear becomes a system.
> A system becomes a country.
> A country remembers the relationship that created it.

Do not make the game bigger unless it makes the core sharper.

Do not make the game darker unless it makes the choices more meaningful.

Do not make the game more complex unless it helps the player feel that their early relationship choices became the later world.
